Tips and Tricks/Troubleshooting

Track how much your dough has risen

Track how far your dough has risen by cutting off a little chunk (after you've mixed the ingredients and let it settle for an hour or so) and putting it in a small, transparent container (like a shotglass with flat sides). Flatten the dough, cover the top, and mark the height of the dough with a rubber band. This way you can easily see when the dough has expanded by about 40-50% from it's starting size, at which point it will be ready for final proofing. The nice thing about doing it this way is that you don't have to worry about the exact temperature or timing (which will vary from day-to-day anyway); instead, you just check on your little dough sample whenever you stretch and fold, and when it's risen enough, you're done!

Control the fermentation speed

You can speed up your fermentation by putting it somewhere warm; usually you can just put it in the oven with the light on to provide a little bit of heat. Some ovens will warm the dough too much, so make sure to check on the temperature and rise every half hour or so. This is a nice trick when it's getting cold and late and it looks like the dough won't rise enough for final proofing before bed.

Make sure the dough can breath/dry during final proofing

If you don't have a bread basket, make sure to put some kind of cloth on the bottom of your bowl to wick away moisture from the dough. Since sourdgough has a lot of water in it, it needs a bit of firmness in the skin (like a water baloon) to keep it's shape (or else it will flatten like a pancake when you take it out to bake). Also make sure not to fully cover the top (with saran wrap or the like); putting a small plate on top should allow enough drying to give you a firm, workable skin.
